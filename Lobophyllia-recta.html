Sinuous Cup Coral

×

Scientific Name

Lobophyllia recta (Dana, 1846)

Common Name

Sinuous Cup Coral

Order

Scleractinia

Family

Lobophylliidae

IUCN Red List Category

Least Concern

Distinguishing Morphological Features

Formation: hemispherical to flat. Valley: 12 to 15 mm wide, highly sinuous. Polyp: walls have moderately thick fleshy appearance, often with a groove along the top. Color: brown, gray, or green; either mottled or with walls and valleys in contrasting colors.

Biology

Marine. Occurs in upper reef slopes and fringing reefs .Inhabits a wide range of depths and may be more resistant to increased temperatures

Locality collected from

Not Available

Distribution

Australia, India, Indian Ocean, Indonesia, Madagascar, Mozambique, Papua New Guinea, Republic of Mauritius, Seychelles, South Pacific Ocean, United States, Indo-Pacific: Yemen, Maldives to Melanesia, north to Japan and south to Australia.
